Brief Background

The IPPT, or Individual Physical Proficiency Test, is a standardized physical fitness test used by the Singapore Armed Forces (SAF), Singapore Police Force (SPF) and the Singapore Civil Defence Force (SCDF) to assess the physical fitness of their members.

IPPT is applicable to all medically-eligible persons with National Service (NS) liability which includes career professionals in these 3 forces, active conscripts and reservist personnel (or more commonly known as National Servicemen or NSMen).

Scoring

It consists of 3 stations - push-ups, sit-ups and the 2.4km run. The push-ups and sit-ups stations each have a 1 minute time limit.
Push-ups and sit-ups stations each have a maximum attainable score of 25, while the 2.4km run station has a maximum attainable score of 50.
The maximum attainable score for the IPPT is 100.

The scoring is normalized via an age group system where younger and presumably fitter persons are in a lower age group and older people are in a higher age group.
For example, if you are in age group 1 (the youngest age group for anyone below 22 years old), to get a maximum score of 25 in push-ups, you will need to do 60 reps in 1 minute.
However, if you were in age group 10 (ages 46-48), to get the same score, you will need to do just 49 push-ups in 1 minute.
A similar age-based score system is applied for the other 2 stations as well where the requirements to get a higher score is tougher for younger persons.

Award and Incentive System

To incentivise people with NS liability to maintain their physical fitness, the Singapore Government has rolled out the IPPT Incentive Awards to provide monetary awards of varying amounts according to an individual's IPPT performance.

This can only be claimed once every year for each eligible individual (i.e. full-time/career members of the three forces and eligible reservist personnel).

For example, if you took a test in March 2023 and attained the Silver award, you will receive $300.
If you decide to attempt again at a later date, say sometime in June 2023, and you attain the Gold award, you will receive $200 to reflect the improvement from Silver to Gold (the Gold award is $500).

There is no penalty if you attain a lower award type in a subsequent attempt (i.e. you will not need to pay back the difference if you attain an award lower than your initial attempt in subsequent attempts).
